General IPO Standards

Application & Offer Structure

  • Capital: Existing Paid-Up Capital must be Tk. 30 Crore.
  • Offer Size: Min. 10% of Post IPO Paid-Up Capital or Tk. 30 Crore at par.
  • Minimum Post IPO Paid Up Capital: Tk. 50 Crore.
  • Underwriting: 35% on a firm commitment basis.
  • Post-IPO Limit: Standard limit is Tk. 125 Crore.
  • Sponsor, Sponsor Director Shareholding: Minimum 30% of Post IPO Paid Up Capital.
  • No material Change: No Change after the Submitted Audit Financial Statement attached in Prospectus.
  • Utilise of Previously Raise Capital:At Least 90% of Previouly raised Paid Up Capital have to be utilised.
  • Clear CIB: All Director & Shareholder more than 10% must not be Loan Defulter.
  • Examption of Minimum %: Existing Paid Up Capital/Net worth is more than 500 Crore may be applied of 5% of Post IPO Paid Up Capital.

Note: All applications require Board of Directors' approval regarding Size & Method.

Financial Integrity

Accounting, Integrity & Governance Standards

Capital & Operational Integrity

  • Capital Proof: Banker’s certificate and statements proving deposit of total paid-up capital.
  • Recent Raise: No capital raise (except bonus) within 2 years preceding application.
  • Governance: Regular AGMs and strict compliance with Commission CG Guidelines.
  • Asset Valuation: Mandatory compliance with BSEC valuation guidelines.
  • Cost Audit: Must be obtained if required by the Companies Act, 1994.
  • History: Full capital raising history since inception must be submitted.

Profitability & Reporting

  • Existing Firms: Must show positive net profit after tax for at least the latest year.
  • New Firms: Must demonstrate positive projected net profit after tax.
  • Audit Rigor: BSEC panel auditor must use IFRS/IAS and BSA standards.
  • Vendor Docs: Vendor agreements must be certified by RJSC.
  • Prospectus: Must follow all requirements of the Rules in preparation.

Offering at Premium

Additional Criteria for Premium Issuance

Operational & Financial Thresholds

  • Operational History: Must have been in commercial operation for at least 3 years.
  • Performance: Net profit after tax AND positive net operating cash flows for the preceding 2 years.
  • Credit Rating: Minimum 'A' category in the long-term scale from a BSEC-registered agency.
  • Offer Price: Cut off Price Only.

Conflict of Interest Controls

  • IM Connection: The Issue Manager or its connected persons cannot be connected with the issuer or hold its securities.
  • Exception: Connected persons may bid in Book Building only with prior BSEC approval.

Green Field Companies

Offer Structure & Operational Planning

Offer Structure & Capital

  • Pricing: Must NOT offer shares at a premium.
  • Capital Limit: Post-IPO paid-up capital may exceed Tk. 125 Crore.
  • Min Size: Existing Paid-Up Capital of Tk. 50 Crore required.
  • Min Offer: 10% of post-IPO capital or Tk. 75 Crore (higher of).
  • Rating: Must maintain 'A' Category in Long-Term scale.

Operational Plan

  • Detailed plan of operation and EPC contract (if necessary) must be in place.
  • Positive projected net profit for next 3 years, audited by BSEC panel auditors.

Sponsor & Equity Commitment

  • 75% Equity: Sponsors/Group/Directors must hold min 75% of post-IPO capital.
  • Retention: Must retain this 75% until NPAT is positive for 2 consecutive years.
  • Sponsor Track Record: Must show success in listed/industrial units.
  • Track Record Exemptions: Provisions (11,12,13) do not apply to PPP or state/foreign majority firms.

Repeat Public Offer (RPO)

Disclosure, Approvals & Compliance

Information Disclosure & PSI

  • PSI Dissemination: RPO details must be released as Price Sensitive Information immediately upon Board decision, General Meeting approval, and BSEC approval.
  • Explicit Announcement: Initial PSI (Board/General Meeting) must explicitly state RPO is subject to Commission approval.
  • Formal Approvals: Mandatory approval required from Board of Directors, Shareholders (General Meeting), and BSEC.

Compliance & Financials

  • Fund Utilization: At Least 90% of previous public offer or rights issue proceeds must be utilized with reports submitted to BSEC.
  • Underwriting: 35% of the issue must be underwritten on a firm commitment basis.
  • Credit Rating: Issuer must hold at least an Investment Grade rating from a registered rating agency.

Roles & Responsibilities

Issue Management & "Expert" Accountability

Appointments & Structural Roles

  • Issue Manager (IM): Mandatory appointment of a BSEC-registered entity.
  • Registrar: The IM may act as Registrar or appoint an eligible entity. Note: The Stock Exchange ESS is recognized to perform Registrar functions.
  • IM Core Duties: Responsible for Prospectus preparation, conducting the Road Show, and monitoring fund utilization.
  • Reporting: IM must submit a monthly utilization report and declaration to BSEC within 10 days of month-end.

Legal Status as "Expert"

The IM, Registrar, Valuer, Auditor, and Credit Rating Co. are deemed "Experts" under:

  • Sections 136 & 137 of the Companies Act, 1994.
  • Section 34 of the Financial Reporting Act, 2015.
Accountability: Experts are liable for due diligence certificates, document authenticity, and any statements on the merit of the issue.

Underwriting Framework

Commitments & Handling Under-Subscription

Appointment & Obligation

  • Appointment: One or more BSEC-registered underwriters must be appointed.
  • Core Accountability: Lead underwriters are responsible for pre-issue, public issue, and post-issue legal compliance.

The 15-Day Payment Rule

In case of under-subscription, the "Firm Commitment" triggers:

  • Notice: Issuer sends notice within 10 days of subscription closure.
  • Subscription: Underwriter must subscribe and pay cash in full within 15 days of notice.
  • Credit: Funds must be credited to the subscription account within the same window.

Underwriting Workflow

Reporting to Commission:
The Issuer/Lead IM must provide proof of underwriter deposit to BSEC within 7 days after the payment deadline.

Application Procedure

Submission, Documentation & Regulatory Review

Submission & Timing

  • Simultaneous Filing: Application must be submitted to BSEC for consent and Stock Exchange(s) for listing at the same time.
  • Book Building Rule: The Road Show must be conducted before the final application is submitted.
  • FS Validity: Audited Financial Statements must not be older than 120 days at the time of submission.
  • Immediate Posting: Draft/Red-herring prospectus must be hosted on the Issuer's and Issue Manager's websites immediately.

Documentation Standards

  • Mandatory Signatories: Each page must be signed by the Issuer (CEO/MD, CFO, Secretary) and the Issue Manager (CEO/MD).
  • Annexure Compliance: Full submission of Annexures A, C, D, E, F, G (and Annexure J for Preference Shares).

Regulatory Review & Amendments

Information Requests:
The Commission or Exchanges may request:
• Additional disclosures or clarifications.
• Certifications from experts (Auditors/Valuers).
• Data from other government authorities (via BSEC).
Amendments:
Any changes to the prospectus must be signed by mandatory signatories and re-filed with BSEC and the Exchanges.
Compliance Deadline: All parties must fulfill regulatory requirements within the stipulated timeframe or face delay/rejection.

Road Show Procedure

Institutional Price Discovery & RHP Preparation

Invitation & Red-Herring Prospectus

  • Public Notice: Publication in 3 national dailies (2 Bangla, 1 English) with 10 working days' notice.
  • Disclosure: Upload RHP to website including 3 years of Audited FS and 3 years of Projected FS.
  • RHP Content: Must exclude final price/quantity but include Annexure C valuation methods and detailed calculations.
  • Observers: Representatives from Stock Exchanges must be present as observers.

Eligible Investor (EI) Input

  • Valuation Submission: EIs must submit their own price/quantity justifications within 3 days of the road show.
  • Statistical Summary: RHP must include a summary of EI valuations (Mean, Median, 5th Percentile).

Indicative Price Finalization

Pricing Framework:
Indicative Price must be supported by:
• 4 Methods: 2 Absolute + 2 Relative (Annexure C).
• 40 Institutional Valuations (at least):
• 30 EIs (Portfolio Mgrs, Dealers, Asset Mgrs), Each min. 10 on each category
• 10 EIs (Banks & others).
Final Step: Following the road show and price finalization, the Final Application is filed simultaneously with BSEC and the Exchanges.

Stock Exchange Recommendation

Review, Public Comment & Final Filing

Public Comment Phase

  • Notification: Exchange uploads RHP and notifies Eligible Investors (EIs).
  • EI Comments: EIs have 7 days to provide feedback.
  • Processing: Exchange shares comments with the Issuer within 3 days; Issuer has 7 days to respond/satisfy the Exchange.

Internal & Financial Review

  • Methodology: Review conducted by the Exchange, a specialized panel, or a BSEC-panel auditor.
  • Timeline: Review must be completed within 30 days of application receipt.
  • Clarifications: The Exchange may request additional data from any party (Auditor, Valuer, Underwriter, etc.).

Final Recommendation

Submission to BSEC:
• Deadline: Within 30 days of application receipt.
• Content: Formal recommendation/rejection (Annexure-B), findings, and checklist.
• Extension: BSEC may grant a 15-day extension upon request.
• Physical Visit: Exchanges may visit Issuer Factory & Office.
Note: In case of positive recommendation, the final version of the RHP must be sent to the Commission immediately.

Slide 15

Bidding & Subscription

ESS Execution, Cut-Off Price & Allotment

Bidding Rules (ESS)

  • Duration: 72 hours round-the-clock via automated system.
  • Price Band: ±25% of the Indicative Price.
  • Limits: Max bid 1% of EI portion; 5% variation allowed on one bid revision.
  • Deposit: Full bid amount must be deposited in advance.

Price Determination

  • Cut-Off Price: The nearest integer price where the total EI portion is exhausted (recorded in descending order).
  • Allotment: Pro-rata at the cut-off price for all bids at or above the cut-off.
  • Transfer: Funds to Issuer within 5 days; excess refunds to EIs within 7 days.

Integrity & Penalties

Anti-Collusion: Cartels and tacit collusion are strictly prohibited.
• BSEC may cancel bids or bar EIs for the next 3 public offers.
• Connected parties must provide a Non-Collusion Declaration.
General Public Offer: Conducted at the Cut-Off Price after final prospectus approval. Minimum 10% post-IPO capital rule applies.
BSEC Public Issue Rules 2025
Slide 16

EI Subscription Process

Fixed Price Method Execution

Consent & Subscription Rules

  • Regulatory Prerequisite: BSEC issues final consent and approves the prospectus only after a positive recommendation from the exchange(s).
  • ESS System: Conducted via the Stock Exchange's uniform automated system.
  • Window: Open for 120 hours round-the-clock.
  • Financials: Full subscription amount must be deposited in advance; individual EI subscription is capped at 1% of the EI portion.

Allotment & Transfers

  • Logic: Pro-rata allotment in case of over-subscription.
  • Timeline: Allotment list posted within 3 working days; funds transferred to issuer within 5 working days.
  • General Public: Subscription for general investors opens only after the EI phase is finalized.

Contingency & Under-subscription

Under-subscription Critical Thresholds:
• Up to 35%: The unsubscribed EI portion is transferred to the General Investor category.
• Above 35%: The entire issue is cancelled.
Refunds: Any excess subscribed amounts must be refunded to EIs within 7 working days of subscription closure.

Commission Review & Decision

BSEC Approval, Rejection & Withdrawal Protocols

BSEC Decision Timeline

  • Basis of Review: Decision is based on the application, Exchange recommendation, findings, and compliance checklist.
  • Timeline: BSEC must issue a Letter of Consent or a written Rejection within 25 days of receiving the Exchange's recommendation.
  • Material Defects: If BSEC finds misrepresentations in Exchange findings, it may request an Exchange review within 7 days.
  • Final Authority: BSEC reserves absolute discretion to accept, reject, or suspend issues in the interest of the capital market.

Review & Withdrawal

  • Appeals: Issuers may apply for a review within 30 days of rejection; the Commission’s final decision requires a hearing for all concerned parties.
  • Withdrawal: An issuer may withdraw its application anytime before prospectus issuance with Board approval.

Prospectus Formatting

Mandatory Formats:
• Full Prospectus/RHP: Must follow Annexure–H
• Abridged Version: Must follow Annexure–I.
Information Requests:
BSEC may request additional clarification or documents from any party (Auditor, Valuer, IM, etc.) during the review period.

Publication & Accessibility

Under-Subscription Protocol:
• Notice: Issuer notifies underwriters within 10 days of closure.

Final Regulatory Provisions

Integrity, Authority & Transition to 2025 Rules

Integrity & Penalties

  • Prohibition: No person shall submit any info, report, or due diligence certificate that is known to be false or untrue in any material particular.
  • BSEC Enforcement: Contravention, including suppression of facts or misleading statements, triggers penal actions under the Securities and Exchange Ordinance, 1969.

Commission's Finality

Final Authority: In case of any ambiguity, confusion, or difference of opinion regarding these Rules, the decision of the Commission (BSEC) shall be final and binding on all parties.

Repeal & Savings

  • Repeal: The Public Issue Rules, 2015 are hereby repealed.
  • Transition: Applications submitted under the 2015 Rules are now treated as filed under the 2025 Rules. Issuers must update applications to meet new requirements.
  • Savings Clause: Consents, fees, and resolutions under the 2015 Rules remain valid as if done under the new framework.
Note: All concerned parties (Issuers, IMs, Auditors) must ensure immediate compliance with updated disclosure and fee structures.

Valuation Justification

Pricing Framework & Regulatory Constraints

Mandatory Valuation Mix

The Issue Manager must justify the price using at least four (4) methods:

Category Recognized Methods
Absolute (Min. 2) DCFM, DDM, Residual Income (RIM), Asset Based Model
Relative (Min. 2) P/E, P/B, P/Sales, P/EBITDA (for capital intensive firms)

Methodology Rules

  • Fixed Price Cap: Offer price cannot exceed the Net Asset Value (NAV) per share at historical/current cost.
  • Book Building: Indicative price must be justified by the full 4-method suite.
  • Revaluation: Any asset revaluation must follow specific BSEC guidelines.

Constraint Guidelines

Absolute Constraints:
  • Discount Rate: Min. 10-year Treasury Bond yield.
  • Growth Rate: Max. avg growth of last 5 years.
Relative Constraints:
  • Peers: Must use legitimate local or US SEC (SIC Code) classifications.
  • P/E Ratio: Avg of last 12 months' Industry P/E or Market P/E (whichever is lower).

Note: If alternative methods are used, the Issue Manager must adopt a strictly conservative approach.

Anatomy of a Prospectus

Core Disclosures & Legal Framework

1. Executive & Legal Foundation

  • Executive Summary: High-level overview of the offer.
  • BSEC Conditions: Full text of conditions imposed in the Consent Letter.
  • Due Diligence (Annexures): Mandatory declarations by the Issuer (A), Issue Manager (B), and Underwriters (C).

2. Issuer Profile & Governance

  • Corporate Directory: Details of the Issuer and contractual parties.
  • Management: Profiles of Directors, Shariah Board (if any), and Key Officers.
  • Governance: Ownership structure and Corporate Governance Compliance Declaration.
  • Compensation: Executive pay and options granted to staff/officers.

3. Business & Financial Analysis

  • ● Business Description: Detailed operational plan and history.
  • ● Management Discussion (MD&A): In-depth analysis of financial condition and results.
  • ● Related Party Transactions: Disclosures of relationships with Directors and affiliates.
  • ● Material Contracts: Review of significant legal obligations and litigations.
